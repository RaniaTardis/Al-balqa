FAQs

Find answers to your questions about visiting Balqa

What is the best time to visit Balqa?

The best time to visit Balqa is during spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November). The weather is mild and pleasant, perfect for exploring outdoor sites. Summers can be hot, especially in the Jordan Valley (like the Dead Sea), while higher elevations like As-Salt are cooler. Winters can be cold and rainy in the mountains.

What are the must-see attractions in Balqa?

Key attractions include:

  • As-Salt: A UNESCO World Heritage city known for its unique architecture and history of tolerance. Don't miss Hammam Street.
  • Baptism Site (Al-Maghtas): Believed to be the site of Jesus' baptism.
  • Dead Sea: The lowest point on Earth, famous for its buoyant, mineral-rich waters.
  • Roman Tombs in Wadi Shuaib: Ancient historical sites.
  • Zai National Park: For nature lovers and hiking.

Check our Blog page for more details on landmarks.

Do I need a visa to visit Jordan?

Most nationalities require a visa to enter Jordan. Visas can often be obtained upon arrival at Queen Alia International Airport (Amman) and some land borders. However, it's always best to check the latest visa requirements with the Jordanian embassy or consulate in your country before traveling. The "Jordan Pass" can be a cost-effective option as it includes the visa fee (for eligible nationalities staying 3+ nights) and entry to many tourist sites.

Is Balqa safe for tourists? What about dress code?

Jordan, including Balqa, is generally a very safe country for tourists with low crime rates. Standard travel precautions are advised.

Regarding dress code, Jordan is a predominantly Muslim country. While it's generally tolerant, it's respectful to dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites or rural areas. For women, this means covering shoulders and knees. Men should also avoid very short shorts in conservative areas. Swimwear is appropriate at hotel pools and Dead Sea resorts.

How can I get around in Balqa?

Options for getting around Balqa include:

  • Rental Car: Offers the most flexibility for exploring at your own pace. Roads are generally good.
  • Taxis: Available in cities like As-Salt. Agree on a fare beforehand or ensure the meter is used. Ride-hailing apps are also available in larger areas.
  • Buses: Local buses connect towns, but can be infrequent and schedules may not be fixed. More suitable for budget travelers with flexible itineraries.
  • Private Driver/Tours: Many tour operators offer guided tours or private drivers. This can be convenient for visiting multiple sites.
